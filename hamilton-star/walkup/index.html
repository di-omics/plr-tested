walk-up.

live · drives the arm
This is not the simulator. The sibling pages ampseq-run-app*.html are badged SIMULATION and cannot reach the Pi. This one really moves metal. A human stays at the deck with a hand near the E-stop for the whole run. Nothing here changes that.

1 · pick the build

Only builds that have passed on hardware are offered.

2 · samples

Comma separated. Logged with the run so you can trace a plate back later. Optional, and it does not change what the robot does.

3 · deck staging

Look at the deck and confirm each one. Getting these wrong is how an iSWAP releases a plate into open space. This checklist is the gate: the confirm token is not released until every box is ticked by someone who looked.

4 · arm and run

Hold the button for 2 seconds. That hold replaces typing --confirm RUN_AMPSEQ_ODTC_LIDDED_FULL. It is deliberate.

running

What stop does. It signals the runner between legs. The leg in flight finishes its motion, then nothing further launches. It does not freeze the arm mid-transfer, because aborting mid-leg is what strands a plate. If you need the arm to stop now, hit the E-stop, then use recover_*.py to bring the plate home.

This abort path has not yet been exercised on hardware. Treat the E-stop as the real stop.
